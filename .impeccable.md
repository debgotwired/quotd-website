## Design Context

### Users
B2B marketing teams — content marketers, demand gen leads — who own case study production. They're busy, not designers, and need polished customer proof assets fast. They're evaluating Quotd as a tool that replaces the painful process of scheduling interviews, transcribing, and writing up case studies manually. When they land on the site, they want to quickly understand what Quotd does, see that it's credible, and try it.

### Brand Personality
**Refined, confident, quiet.** Monaco/Aesop energy — luxury restraint that lets the work speak. The site should feel like walking into a well-designed space where everything is intentional and nothing is competing for attention. Confidence through understatement, not volume.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Monochrome only. Light mode only. No accent colors. Warmth comes from typography (serif/sans pairing) and generous whitespace, not color.
- **Typography**: Playfair Display for editorial authority on headings. Geist Sans for clean, modern body text. The serif/sans contrast IS the design.
- **References**: monaco.com (monochrome restraint, serif+sans, staggered animation), shiori.sh (clean minimal sections), gong.io (product showcase, feature communication)
- **Anti-references**: NO startup-y/playful (no illustrations, gradients, emoji, fun energy). NO enterprise/cluttered (no mega-navs, feature dumps, Salesforce vibes). NO generic SaaS template (no hero-stats-features-pricing-footer cookie cutter). It should feel like its own thing.
- **Logo**: "The Stack" — stacked case study pages with quote mark + verified badge. Exists in light and dark SVG variants.

### Design Principles
1. **Restraint is the luxury** — Every element must earn its place. When in doubt, remove. White space is not empty space, it's breathing room.
2. **Typography does the heavy lifting** — Size, weight, and serif/sans contrast create hierarchy. Don't rely on icons, colors, or decorative elements to communicate.
3. **Break the template** — Vary section rhythm, alignment, and density. If every section looks the same, the page becomes wallpaper. Asymmetry and surprise signal design intention.
4. **Show the product** — A case study tool that doesn't show a case study is a red flag. The deliverable IS the proof.
5. **Quiet confidence** — No exclamation marks, no "revolutionary", no hype language. State what it does simply and let the reader draw their own conclusions.

### Accessibility
Good contrast and font sizes. No specific WCAG compliance target, but should be readable and navigable by keyboard. Respect reduced-motion preferences.

### Technical Constraints
- Next.js 16 / React 19 / Tailwind 4
- Static site (no server components needed for marketing page)
- Auto-deploys via Vercel on push to main
- Domain: quotd.sh (marketing), app.quotd.sh (product)
